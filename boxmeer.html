Boxmeer, 1400

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Boxmeer (Netherlands) occurred in 1400 when consecrated wine transformed into physical blood, bubbling out of the chalice and coagulating onto the altar cloth (corporal). It is one of the celebrated events documented by Blessed Carlo Acutis's exhibition.

The Miracle Event

  • The Setting: St. Peter and Paul Church (now a Basilica) in Boxmeer, Netherlands.
  • The Crisis: During the consecration, Father Arnoldus Groen experienced a profound doubt about the True Presence of Christ in the Eucharist.
  • The Phenomenon: Suddenly, the consecrated wine began to boil and bubble in the chalice, overflowing onto the corporal. The wine visibly changed into real blood and coagulated into a solid clump the size of a walnut.
  • The Resolution: Overcome with fear and remorse, the priest begged for forgiveness, and the bubbling ceased.

Veneration & Preservation

The Relics: The blood-stained corporal and the solidified blood have been preserved for centuries.

Devotion: Popes Clement XI, Benedict XIV, Pius IX, and Leo XIII all showed personal devotion to the miracle. The anniversary of the miracle is honored annually with solemn processions through the town.
