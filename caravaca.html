Caravaca de la Cruz, 1231

← Back to Miracles

The 1231 Eucharistic miracle of Caravaca de la Cruz, Spain, occurred when angels brought a True Cross relic to a Mass being celebrated for a local Muslim ruler, who then witnessed the appearance of the Child Jesus within the consecrated Host.

The Miraculous Event

A captive priest was ordered by the ruler Zeyt-Abu-Zeyt to celebrate Mass. Lacking a crucifix, the priest was miraculously provided with a double-barred cross by angels. During the consecration of the Host, the ruler witnessed a beautiful child appearing inside it, an event that led him and his entire court to convert to Christianity.

Legacy

The town was renamed Caravaca de la Cruz in honor of the relic. The double-barred Caravaca Cross became an iconic symbol of faith. The site is now a major pilgrimage destination, and the Vatican permits it to hold a Perpetual Jubilee Year every seven years, making it one of only five such cities in the world.
