Asti, 1535

← Back to Miracles

On July 25, 1535, a Eucharistic miracle occurred at the Collegiate Church of San Secondo in Asti, Italy, when Father Domenico Occelli saw a consecrated Host turn red with living blood during Mass.

The Event

  • The Manifestation: Just before Holy Communion, Father Occelli noticed the Host become streaked with living blood, with three drops falling into the chalice and a fourth clinging to the edge of the Host.
  • The Response: Astonished, the priest invited the attending congregation to come to the altar and witness the bleeding Host.
  • The Resolution: When Father Occelli went to consume the Host, the blood vanished, and the bread instantly returned to its normal white color.

Impact and Papal Recognition

This miraculous sign moved several soldiers in the congregation to convert to the Catholic faith. On November 6, 1535, Pope Paul III issued a Papal Brief authorizing a plenary indulgence for anyone who visits the Church of San Secondo on the anniversary of the miracle to pray for the intentions of the Pope.
