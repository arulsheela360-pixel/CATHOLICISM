Bettbrunn, 1125

← Back to Miracles

The Eucharistic Miracle of Bettbrunn (Germany) occurred in 1125 when a pious farmer, unable to regularly attend church, secretly stole a consecrated Host to keep in his farmhouse.

What Happened

  • The Theft: A zealous but misguided farmer from Viehbrunn (near Bettbrunn) stole a Host from his distant parish church and brought it home for devotion.
  • The Drop: While on his farm, he accidentally dropped the Host onto the ground.
  • The Unmovable Host: Neither the farmer nor anyone else was able to pick the Host up until the Bishop of Regensburg intervened. The Bishop was only able to lift it after promising to construct a church dedicated to the Blessed Sacrament on that exact site.
  • Legacy: The resulting church and the village of Bettbrunn grew into a significant pilgrimage destination.
